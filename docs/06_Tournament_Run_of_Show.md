## 1. Format at a glance

Four consecutive Tuesday nights of group-match pinball on a rotating set of eight machines. Players accumulate points across the season. After four weeks, the eight highest on the ladder play a finals bracket on the last night. Trophy, three cash-equivalent bar tabs, name on the wall.

Designed for a venue running its first organised pinball league, not a sanctioned pro tournament. Easy to learn, easy to run, hard to game. Scales from 8 to 40 players without breaking. If Season 1 works, we apply for IFPA sanctioning ahead of Season 2.

## 2. Eligibility and signup

**Eligibility:** 18+. Beercade is a licensed venue. No exceptions. ID checked at the door same as any other shift.

**Capacity:** 8 minimum, 32 ideal, 40 hard cap. Below 8 players we run as casual matchplay with bar prizes. Above 40 we run a waitlist and promote them in if no-shows free up a slot by 6:45 pm.

**Cost:** $15 per night, paid at the bar on arrival. Includes one drink token (any tap beer or non-alcoholic, value capped at $10). Players can sign up to any combination of the three qualifying nights. Their best 2 nights count toward the ladder. They miss a night, they zero that week.

**Signup channels:**

1. Website tournament page (built into the launch grid, `/league`).
2. In-person at the bar.
3. DM to the Beercade Instagram (manager-shift staff redirects to website).

**Registration software:** Matchplay.events (free tier, well above what we'll need for Season 1). Players create their own profile and tag themselves to "Beercade League Season 1". Roger has admin access. Software handles group draws, score entry, standings, tiebreakers.

**Registration closes:** 6:30 pm sharp on each qualifying night. Late arrivals can play casual on free machines but don't earn ladder points that night.

## 4. Match format

### 4.1 Group matchplay

Players are randomly drawn into groups of four for each round (or groups of three if the player count doesn't divide cleanly). Each group plays one full game on one assigned machine. Three rounds per qualifying night. Groups are re-drawn between rounds — players don't play the same group twice in a season unless mathematically unavoidable.

### 4.2 Points per match

Standard matchplay scoring:

| Finish in 4-player group | Points |
|---|---|
| 1st | 4 |
| 2nd | 2 |
| 3rd | 1 |
| 4th | 0 |

| Finish in 3-player group | Points |
|---|---|
| 1st | 4 |
| 2nd | 2 |
| 3rd | 0 |

Three rounds per night, so max 12 points per night, max 24 points across a player's best two nights. Player's total = sum of their best 2 of 3 qualifying nights. Missed nights count as zero.

### 4.3 Game rules

- **Balls per game:** machine default (3 in nearly all cases).
- **Player order:** by draw position in the group (1st drawn plays first).
- **Tilt:** loss of ball only. No ball-save grants on a tilt.
- **Slam tilt:** loss of entire game. Player scores 0 for that match. The TD can override if the slam was clearly mechanical fault (rare, judgement call).
- **Stuck balls:** player calls TD before any further plunge. TD assesses — usually a nudge resolves it. If not, TD invokes the machine's stuck-ball compensation if available, or grants a re-ball at TD's discretion.
- **Beverages on machines:** absolutely not. One spill voids the machine for the night. House rule, no exceptions.
- **Phones:** no consulting strategy guides or rule cards mid-game. Watching is fine.
- **Coaching:** spectators can heckle, cheer, and laugh. They cannot tell the player what shot to make. One warning, then the spectator is asked to step back.

### 4.4 Catastrophic machine fault

If a machine dies mid-match (power cut, multiple stuck balls, lost ball detection, anything that breaks the game beyond TD recovery):

1. TD freezes scores.
2. The match is rerolled to the next available machine in the pool, with the same group.
3. Scores from the broken game are voided. Group restarts from ball one on the replacement machine.

If no replacement machine is available in the pool, TD awards the match by current rank at the moment of the failure, applying matchplay points as if the game had finished there.

### 4.5 Tiebreakers (end of season)

If two or more players are tied on cumulative points after Week 3:

1. Head-to-head record across the season.
2. Highest single-game score posted by either player at any point in the season.
3. One-ball playoff on a machine chosen by the TD at the start of Week 4.

## 5. The ladder

The ladder is the public standings table, updated after each Tuesday night and posted to the Beercade Instagram Story and the /league page on the website.

**Live standings during a night:** Matchplay.events updates automatically as TD enters scores. A second screen (the iPad mounted near the bar) mirrors the standings so players can see where they sit between rounds.

**Top 8 advance to finals.** After Week 3, the eight players with the highest cumulative points (best 2 of 3 nights) qualify for the Week 4 finals bracket.

If the cut is contested (a tie for the 8th seed), tiebreakers in section 4.5 apply.

## 6. Week 4 finals format

Eight players, single-elimination, two-game cumulative scoring per round.

### 6.1 Bracket structure

```
Quarterfinals (4 matches)
  Seed 1 vs Seed 8
  Seed 4 vs Seed 5
  Seed 2 vs Seed 7
  Seed 3 vs Seed 6

Semifinals (2 matches)
  Winner QF1 vs Winner QF2
  Winner QF3 vs Winner QF4

Final (1 match)
  Winner SF1 vs Winner SF2

3rd place playoff (1 match)
  Loser SF1 vs Loser SF2
```

### 6.2 Per-match format in finals

Each match is two games on two different machines, chosen by the higher seed from the night's available pool. Cumulative score across the two games. Highest total wins.

If tied after two games, sudden-death single-ball playoff on a third machine chosen by the lower seed.

## 7. Prizes

### 7.1 Weekly prizes (Weeks 1-3)

- **Highest single-game score of the night:** $50 bar tab + name on the high-score Story posted Wednesday morning.
- **Most matchplay points on the night:** $50 bar tab.

If the same player wins both, they take both. If a player's score is contested or their conduct is sub-standard during the night (see section 9.4), the TD can decline the award and roll it to the next eligible player.

### 7.2 Season prizes (Week 4)

| Place | Prize |
|---|---|

| Runner-up | $100 bar tab + name on the plaque |

The plaque is mounted on the wall behind the league standings board. New plate engraved each season.

### 7.3 Bar tab terms

Bar tabs are valid for 90 days from issue. Transferable but not redeemable for cash. Not valid on functions or pre-booked private events. Voided if the recipient is ejected from the venue for any reason in that 90-day window.

## 8. Night-of run sheet

Same structure for each Tuesday. Times shift slightly on finals night (section 6.3).

### 8.1 Pre-shift (5:30 pm)

- **5:30 pm:** TD arrives. Roger or designated TD opens up.
- **5:35 pm:** TD walks every machine in the night's pool. Checks: flippers responsive, tilt sensors calibrated, ball-trough loading, displays readable, audio at correct volume. Anything off goes on the "needs fix before 7" list.
- **5:50 pm:** TD opens Matchplay.events, confirms tournament is open, prints two paper backup bracket sheets (in case of internet outage).
- **5:55 pm:** TD posts "machines tonight" Story to Beercade Instagram with the four machines pictured.

### 8.2 Doors open (6:00 pm)

- **6:00 pm:** Doors open. Bar opens. Manager-shift staff at door checking ID, taking $15 entry, issuing drink tokens, directing players to the iPad to check in on Matchplay.
- **6:15 pm:** TD does a second walk of the machines. Any final fixes.
- **6:25 pm:** TD announces "5 minutes to registration close" over the room.
- **6:30 pm:** Registration closes. TD locks the player list in Matchplay.

### 8.3 Round 1 (6:45-7:45 pm)

- **6:35 pm:** TD generates Round 1 group draws.
- **6:45 pm:** Groups posted on the Round Display Board (whiteboard near the bar). Story posted to Instagram showing the board.
- **7:00 pm:** First ball. Groups play their assigned machine. Each group has one designated scorekeeper (TD's call, usually the most senior or experienced player in the group) who enters scores into Matchplay on the shared iPad when the game ends.
- **7:30 pm:** TD circulates, resolves stuck balls, settles rule questions.
- **7:45 pm:** Round 1 should be complete. Any groups still playing get a 5-minute warning, then a hard call at 7:55 pm — incomplete games are scored at current ball state, applying matchplay points by current rank.

### 8.4 Round 2 (8:00-8:45 pm)

Same flow as Round 1. Groups redrawn. TD posts the new groups to the whiteboard and to the Instagram Story.

### 8.5 Round 3 (9:00-9:45 pm)

Same flow as Rounds 1 and 2.

### 8.6 Wrap (9:45-10:30 pm)

- **9:45 pm:** Final scores entered. TD locks the night.
- **9:55 pm:** TD announces the night's results to the room. Highest single-game score, most matchplay points. Bar tab vouchers handed over in person.
- **10:00 pm:** Updated season ladder posted on the whiteboard and Instagram Story. Names of advancing top 8 (or current top 8) called out.
- **10:00 pm onwards:** Casual play. Bar runs as normal Tuesday operations until close.

### 8.7 Post-shift (after close)

- TD enters the night's clips into the Drive content library (Pillar D folder).
- TD writes one paragraph in the league diary at `04 Functions / Tournament / Season_1_Diary.md` — what worked, what didn't, anything to fix before next week.
- Bar tab vouchers logged in `05 Finance / Tournament_Bar_Tabs_Season_1.xlsx`.

## 9. Roles and responsibilities

### 9.1 Tournament Director (TD)

The single decision-maker for the night. All rule disputes resolved by the TD. TD's word is final, including when the TD is wrong (a tournament without a final arbiter falls apart).

**Primary TD:** Roger.
**Deputy TD:** One of the two manager-shift staff, rotating across the four weeks so both have experience.

Both TD and Deputy are trained on the Matchplay.events admin interface before Week 1.

### 9.2 Door and registration

One manager-shift staff member runs the door from 6:00 to 6:30 pm. Tasks: ID check, take $15, issue drink token, direct to iPad for Matchplay check-in.

### 9.3 Bar

Normal bar operations. League nights run alongside, not instead of, regular Tuesday service. Extra staffing not required for Season 1, reviewed before Season 2 if signups grow.

### 9.4 Conduct standards

Beercade is a bar that hosts a tournament, not a tournament that has a bar. Behaviour standards are the same as any other shift — friendly, no slurs, no aggression, no targeting of other players. The TD has authority to:

- Issue a verbal warning.
- Dock matchplay points for the night.
- Eject from the tournament (and refund or not at TD's discretion).
- Eject from the venue, following standard house procedure.

If a player is ejected during a round, their group plays out as a smaller group (3- or 2-player), with scoring per section 4.2.

## 11. What can go wrong

### 11.1 Fewer than 8 players show up

Run as casual matchplay night. Cancel the league for that week and roll the missed week into a make-up Tuesday at the end of the season. Refund the $15 entry (drink token still applies).

### 11.2 More than 40 players show up

Waitlist active. First 40 confirmed get spots. Waitlist promoted in order of arrival if any of the 40 no-show by 6:30 pm. Beyond that, waitlist players play casual on free machines and get priority signup for the next Tuesday.

### 11.3 Machine fails mid-night

Procedure in section 4.4. TD's call.

### 11.4 Dispute escalates between players

TD authority per section 9.4. If the dispute is between a player and the TD personally, the Deputy TD takes over for the rest of the night and the original TD steps fully out of the dispute.

### 11.5 Internet outage (Matchplay.events down)

Paper bracket sheets printed at 5:50 pm are the backup. TD runs the night on paper, reconciles to Matchplay later.

### 11.6 Customer complaint about noise or crowding

Manager-shift staff handles per normal venue procedure. League continues unless the venue requires the room cleared.

### 11.7 Equipment damage or theft

Standard venue procedure. Police if appropriate. Tournament continues unless the room requires clearing.

### 11.8 Champion is the same person two seasons running

Not a problem. Beercade League Season 1 plaque just has more than one line for that player. If it becomes a pattern of dominance that hurts signups, Roger can introduce handicapping in Season 3.

Per-qualifying-night out-of-pocket: $100. Per-season out-of-pocket: roughly $535 across all four nights.

### 12.2 Per-night revenue

Assuming 24 players average across qualifying nights:

- Entry fees: 24 x $15 = $360
- Drink token cost to the bar (cost not price): 24 x ~$4 = $96
- Net entry revenue: ~$264
- Plus additional bar spend beyond the drink token (estimate $25-40 per player on a league night) = ~$600-960

Tournament is roughly cost-neutral on entry fees alone; the real return is the additional Tuesday-night bar spend and the social/PR halo.

### 12.3 Break-even point

8 paying players covers the night's $100 in bar tabs. Anything beyond that is upside.

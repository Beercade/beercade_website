# 05c — Practical Exercises

**Client:** Beercade Australia
**Owner:** Roger Robertson
**Version:** v1.0
**Date:** Sat 23 May 2026
**Use with:** `05a_Training_Program.md`
**Audience:** Trainee + Roger as reviewer

---

## 1. Purpose

The training sessions cover theory. These exercises cover the only thing that actually matters: did the trainee produce work that meets the rulebook, on a real shift, with the venue full and the bar busy. Two weeks of structured assignments, with a review template Roger uses at the end of every shift.

## 2. Week 1 — Shooting only (no posting)

**Trainee's only job this week is raw footage. None of this goes on the public account during week 1.** Roger reviews every clip in the end-of-shift sit-down, then either approves it for the content library or marks it for re-shoot.

### 2.1 Targets

| Shift | Pillar focus | Clip target | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shift 1 | A — Machines | 3 clips | Includes one "the save" attempt, one score climb, one hands shot |
| Shift 2 | B — Drinks | 3 clips | One pour, one pickup, one drink line-up |
| Shift 3 | C — Vibe | 2 clips | One front-door-at-dusk, one corner-booth-at-peak |
| Shift 4 | Trainee's choice | 2 clips | Any pillar. They pick what's been weakest |
| Shift 5 | Mixed | 2 clips | One Pillar A, one Pillar B. End-of-week test |

Total target: 12 clips over 5 shifts. Realistic minimum: 8. Quality over quantity. A weak clip is worse than no clip.

### 2.2 Process per shift

1. **Before the shift:** trainee writes down on a sticky note which pillar and which shots from the list they're targeting tonight. Stuck on the back-bar where they'll see it.
2. **During the shift:** shoot when natural moments present themselves. Don't stage anything that won't read as real. Don't ask customers to redo things for the camera.
3. **End of shift:** trainee uploads raw footage to Drive at `02 Marketing / Content Library / Raw Footage / YYYY-Wxx / 00_Trainee_Week1`. Files named per convention (`YYYY-MM-DD_pillar-letter_short-description.mp4`).
4. **End of shift sit-down (10 min):** Roger and trainee watch every clip from the shift, on the trainee's phone. Roger fills in the review template (section 4 below). No editing, no captioning this week. Just shooting.

### 2.3 End of week 1 review (30 min)

Roger and trainee sit down with all 8-12 clips. The conversation covers:

1. Which clip is the trainee proudest of, and why?
2. Which clip would Roger never let go public, and why?
3. What was the hardest pillar to shoot, and what's the plan for week 2?
4. One specific framing or lighting habit to fix before week 2.

The end-of-week-1 review is logged in `02 Marketing / Content Library / 99_Training_Log / [Trainee_Name] / Week_1_Review.md`. One paragraph per question. This becomes the trainee's record and Roger's reference if anyone questions the sign-off later.

## 3. Week 2 — Full workflow (shoot, edit, caption, queue, review)

**This week the trainee runs the whole loop, but Roger holds the publish button.** Nothing on the feed or Reels grid goes live without Roger clicking through in Meta Business Suite. Stories go up live but Roger physically watches each one over the trainee's shoulder this week.

### 3.1 Targets

| Shift | Output |
|---|---|
| Shift 1 | 2 clips shot + 1 edited + caption drafted + 1 Story posted live |
| Shift 2 | 2 clips shot + 1 Reel edited + caption drafted + 2 Stories live |
| Shift 3 | 2 clips shot + 1 feed post queued + 1 Reel queued + 3 Stories live |
| Shift 4 | 2 clips shot + 1 feed post queued + 1 Reel queued + 3 Stories live |
| Shift 5 | 1 piece for each of the four pillars, fully captioned and queued + 4 Stories live |

By the end of week 2 the trainee should have queued at least one full pillar set (one A, one B, one C, one D) and posted 13+ Stories live with Roger supervising.

### 3.2 Process per shift

1. **Before the shift:** trainee plans the night on the same sticky-note system as week 1, with one addition — they note the caption angle for at least one of the planned clips.
2. **During the shift:** shoot, then in slow moments edit on the venue's iPad (CapCut). Write captions on the spot, while the moment is fresh.
3. **Queue, don't publish.** Feed posts and Reels go into the Meta Business Suite scheduled queue. The trainee fills in the caption, hashtags, location tag, and time. Then stops.
4. **Stories go live with Roger watching.** Trainee shows Roger the Story on the phone screen, says out loud which checklist points they've cleared, and only then posts.
5. **End of shift sit-down (15 min):** Roger reviews the queued posts. Approves, sends back for revision, or rejects. Approved posts go live at their scheduled time. Review template (section 4) gets filled in.

### 3.3 End of week 2 review (45 min)

The end-of-week-2 review feeds directly into the competency check (`05d_Competency_Check.md`). It's longer because it covers the full workflow.

Walk through:

1. Pillar coverage: did the trainee shoot all four pillars in correct ratios?
2. The three queued posts Roger would be happiest publishing without changes.
3. The one queued post Roger would have killed if it had reached the queue without supervision.
4. Caption quality: did the trainee drift into banned vocabulary? Catch their own mistakes? Get specific?
5. Workflow discipline: file naming, Drive folders, Stories supervised before posting.
6. The trainee's self-assessment: what do they think they still need to learn?

Logged in `02 Marketing / Content Library / 99_Training_Log / [Trainee_Name] / Week_2_Review.md`.

## 5. Bonus assignments (use if a trainee is moving faster than expected)

Optional, only if the trainee is already hitting the standard before week 2 ends. Don't pile these on a struggling trainee.

### B1 — The 30-second silent walk-through

Shoot one continuous 30-second vertical clip walking from the front door to the back of the venue. No cuts. No audio added. The room is the story. Goal: capture all three of front-of-house, machines, and bar in one shot. Hard. Most attempts fail. That's the point.

### B2 — The customer testimonial

Find a regular willing to talk on camera. Forty-five seconds, vertical, one question: "Why do you come here?" Verbal consent recorded at the start of the clip. This is harder than it sounds and worth practising on staff before approaching customers.

### B3 — The before-and-after function

For a confirmed function booking (when the trainee is on shift), shoot the room set up for the function (before guests arrive) and then a tight shot of the booth in use during (no faces). Two clips, edited together as a Reel. Demonstrates Pillar D's specific job: drive bookings.

## 6. What "passing" looks like at the end of week 2

The trainee is ready for the competency check if:

- They produced at least 8 approved clips per week with no repeated rule violations.
- They wrote at least 8 captions in week 2 that passed the banned-word check on first draft.
- They ran the pre-post checklist verbally on every Story in week 2 without prompting.
- They filed everything to Drive with correct naming, on every shift.
- Roger would let them post Stories unsupervised tomorrow.

If any of these is a no, run week 2 again before the competency check. Don't sign someone off who isn't ready — the sign-off is what lets them post live, and a bad sign-off lands on the venue's account.

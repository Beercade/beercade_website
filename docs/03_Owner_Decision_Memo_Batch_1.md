### D3. Pinball tournament format — single-elimination night vs four-week ladder league

The launch tournament is proposed; I need you to pick the format. Two options, costed below.

**Format 1 — Single-elimination, one big Friday night.** 7pm start, 32 entrants, $25 entry, three machines drawn at random per round, trophy plus $300 bar tab to the winner. Done by 11pm. Pros: easy to promote, big spectator energy, simple to run. Cons: half the field is out by 8pm, the room turns over fast, and it doesn't build a regular crowd past launch night.

**Format 2 — Ladder league, four weeks. (Recommended.)** Tuesdays and Thursdays for four weeks, points accumulate, top 8 enter a finals night in week five. $40 covers all sessions. Trophy plus $500 bar tab to the overall winner. Pros: builds a regular crowd at the venue across a month (better revenue), pulls Dave's persona in from out-of-area, produces a content beat for socials every week, finals night becomes its own event. Cons: more operational lift — five touchpoints instead of one — but spread across nights, not crammed into one.

The brand position is the pub night that actually has something to do, not the one-off event venue. A four-week league fits that position better and turns the launch window into a content engine.

**My recommendation: Format 2 (four-week ladder league), starting Tue 16 June.**

Your call: ☐ Format 1 / ☐ Format 2 / ☐ Both (kickoff event week one, league starts week two)

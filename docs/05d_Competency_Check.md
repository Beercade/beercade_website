# 05d — Competency Check

**Client:** Beercade Australia
**Owner:** Roger Robertson
**Version:** v1.0
**Date:** Sat 23 May 2026
**Use with:** `05a_Training_Program.md`, `05c_Practical_Exercises.md`
**Administered by:** Roger
**Duration:** 30 minutes total

---

## 1. Purpose

The sign-off gate. End of week 2 of training. Two parts: a scenario quiz the trainee completes in writing, and a portfolio review where Roger and the trainee look at the two weeks of work together. Pass both, and the trainee is signed off to post Stories without prior approval. Fail either, and the weakest practical week gets re-run before another attempt.

This is not a school exam. The point is to confirm the trainee genuinely sees what's on the rulebook, not just that they can recite it. The scenarios are written to catch the mistakes that real venue accounts actually make.

## 2. Scoring

| Part | Max marks | Pass mark |
|---|---|---|
| Part A — Scenario quiz | 10 | 8/10 |
| Part B — Portfolio review | Pass/refer | Roger's judgement |

A trainee who passes Part A but Roger flags concerns in Part B does not pass overall. Roger's judgement is the final gate.

## 3. Part A — Scenario quiz (20 minutes)

Print this section. Give to the trainee. They write answers in pen on the paper, alone, no notes, no phone. Roger is in the room but not coaching. Twenty minutes. Roger marks immediately after.

For each scenario, the trainee writes:

1. **Verdict:** POST, FIX, or KILL
2. **Reason:** one sentence

### Marker key (Roger reads after the trainee has finished)

**Scenario 1.** FIX. The customer gave qualified consent ("don't make me look ugly"). That's a conditional yes, not a clear yes. Re-frame to hands or back-of-head only, or get a clearer verbal yes before using their face.

**Scenario 2.** KILL or FIX. No consent from identifiable customers. Either kill the clip, or re-shoot from an angle where faces aren't readable, or blur in the edit. Default to kill — easier than fixing.

**Scenario 3.** FIX. The clip is fine. The Spotify audio is the problem. Shoot silent or with machine sound, add a track from Instagram's licensed library in the edit. Spotify in the background gets flagged and the Reel gets muted.

**Scenario 4.** KILL the caption, FIX with new copy. Banned words used: vibes, iconic, experience, best of both worlds. Plus three emoji (we don't ban emoji but they don't help here). Rewrite: name the machine, name the moment, sound like a text. Example: "Multiball on Funhouse. Last train home at 11:47."

**Scenario 5.** FIX. The winner consent is fine. The drunk customer in the background is the problem. Re-frame, crop in post, or re-shoot. Don't post as-is.

**Scenario 6.** FIX. DM the customer first. Quick "hey, mind if we share this to our Story?" then repost only with a yes back. Even though they tagged us, asking takes ten seconds and protects the relationship.

**Scenario 7.** FIX. The booking contract is good, but eight identifiable faces in one frame still warrants a verbal check with the host before posting. Contract language doesn't replace common sense. Default to: ask the host, blur or reframe if hesitation, only post with clear yes.

**Scenario 8.** POST. Pillar B, no people, no minors, audio neutral, caption is specific (names the beer and the time), checklist clears. Ship it.

**Scenario 9.** POST (the clip itself). FIX the workflow — uploads should happen at end of shift, not from home next day. Talk to the trainee about why the timing matters (Roger needs same-night review on training weeks). One-off late upload is forgivable; pattern is not.

**Scenario 10.** KILL. "Happy if this goes on our Instagram" was answered by someone visibly drunk. They can't meaningfully consent. Doesn't matter what they said. Doesn't matter that they were stoked. Bin the clip.

### Marking

| Marks | Outcome |
|---|---|
| 10/10 | Outstanding. Sign off. |
| 8-9/10 | Pass. Sign off. Roger flags the missed scenarios in the portfolio review for follow-up. |
| 6-7/10 | Refer. Re-run weakest week of practical, then re-take quiz with different scenarios (Roger writes new ones). |
| 0-5/10 | Refer. Full re-training. Trainee re-takes both sessions before any more practical work. |

A trainee scoring 8/10 but getting Scenario 1, 5, or 10 wrong (the consent ones) gets a separate sit-down on consent specifically before sign-off. Those three are the highest-stakes rules.

## 4. Part B — Portfolio review (10 minutes)

Roger and trainee, side by side, looking at the two weeks of training-log Drive folder together. Phone or laptop, doesn't matter.

### Roger picks four pieces

Before the sit-down, Roger looks through the training log and picks:

1. The best clip from each pillar (4 total).
2. One piece the trainee queued in week 2 that Roger killed before publishing.

### The conversation

For each of the four best clips, the trainee answers:

1. Which pillar, and why?
2. What rule from the rulebook was hardest to get right in this clip?
3. What would you do differently if you re-shot it tomorrow?

For the killed piece:

1. Why did Roger kill it?
2. What would you have done differently to avoid the kill?

### What Roger is listening for

- The trainee can talk about their own work in terms of the rulebook, not just instinct.
- The trainee can describe a rule violation without getting defensive.
- The trainee has at least one specific thing they want to get better at next month.
- The trainee names the rule, not "I just felt like it didn't work."

If the trainee blanks on the rule-naming question, that's a refer. They can't post unsupervised if they can't articulate the rules.

## 5. Sign-off

If the trainee passes both parts:

1. Roger logs one line in `02 Marketing / Content Library / 00_Trained_Staff.md`:
   ```
   [Trainee Name] — passed competency check [YYYY-MM-DD] — Stories unsupervised, Feed/Reels reviewed for 4 weeks
   ```
2. Roger adds the trainee to the Meta Business Suite admin list.
3. Roger sends a short message in the team chat: "[Trainee] is signed off to post Stories without prior approval as of [date]. Feed and Reels still go through the review queue for the next four weeks. Welcome to the content roster."

### Four weeks later

After four weeks of supervised feed and Reels posting with no incidents, the trainee graduates to full unsupervised posting. Roger reviews the four-week period the same way as the end-of-week-2 review (best three, worst one, what to work on next).

If anything goes wrong during the four-week probation period — a rule violation, a post that has to come down, a customer complaint — the supervised period resets to zero and the trainee re-runs the competency check.

## 6. Refresher cadence

A passed competency check is not permanent. Conditions for re-taking it:

- **Annually:** every staff member re-takes the scenario quiz (with fresh scenarios Roger writes each year). Pass mark stays at 8/10.
- **After a rule violation that required a takedown:** immediate re-take. Doesn't matter how senior the staff member is.
- **When the rulebook changes substantively:** Roger judges if a re-take is warranted. Small wording changes don't need it. Adding a new pillar or changing the don't-post rules does.

# 05a — Staff Content Training Program

**Client:** Beercade Australia
**Owner:** Roger Robertson
**Authors:** John Willsdon (consultant) with Roger
**Version:** v1.0
**Date:** Sat 23 May 2026
**Audience:** Two manager-shift staff
**Facilitator:** Roger (with John on call for the first run)
**Status:** Draft for Roger review

---

## 1. What this is

A two-session training program that takes a new staff member from "I've never shot content for a venue" to "I can produce, caption, and post on-brand work without supervision" inside one working week. Built around the rulebook in `05_Staff_Content_Guidelines.md`. Everything in this program references that document. If a trainee finishes the program and can't quote three of the pre-post checklist items from memory, they're not ready to post yet.

## 2. Training outcomes

By the end of the program, the trainee can:

1. Identify which of the four content pillars any clip belongs to, and explain why.
2. Set up a phone shot correctly for Beercade's lighting without asking.
3. Recognise a post that violates the "what not to shoot" rules before it goes live.
4. Write a caption that passes the voice rules, including the banned-words check.
5. Upload, name, and file footage in the Drive folder structure without prompting.
6. Run the five-point pre-post checklist from memory.

A trainee who can't do all six is not signed off, even if they're enthusiastic and the venue is short-staffed. Bad content is more expensive than no content.

## 3. Program structure

| Session | Duration | When | Format | Lead |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Session 1 — Pillars and limits | 45 min | Trainee's first week, off-shift | Classroom + venue walk | Roger |
| Practical week 1 | 5 shifts | Live shifts | On-the-job shooting | Trainee, reviewed by Roger |
| Session 2 — Craft and posting | 45 min | Start of week 2, off-shift | Classroom + hands-on edit | Roger |
| Practical week 2 | 5 shifts | Live shifts | Full workflow, supervised posting | Trainee, reviewed by Roger |
| Competency check | 30 min | End of week 2 | Scenario quiz + portfolio review | Roger |

Total contact time: roughly 2.5 hours formal training over two weeks, plus normal shift work where the trainee is already on the clock.

## 4. Session 1 — Pillars and limits (45 minutes)

**Goal:** The trainee leaves understanding what we shoot, what we don't, and where it all lives.

**Pre-reading sent the day before:** sections 2, 5, and 6 of `05_Staff_Content_Guidelines.md`.

**Bring:** the trainee's phone, fully charged. A printed copy of `05b_Quick_Reference_Cards.md` cards 1-3.

### Run sheet

**0:00-0:05 — Why content matters here.** Roger explains the no-paid-ads constraint and that the social accounts are the marketing budget. One sentence: every post either brings someone in or fills a function booking, or it doesn't earn its place on the grid.

**0:05-0:15 — The four pillars.** Roger walks the trainee through each pillar (Machines, Drinks, Vibe, Events) by physically pointing at examples in the venue. Stand next to a machine for Pillar A. Stand at the bar for Pillar B. Use the room itself, not slides. Ten minutes covers all four.

**0:15-0:25 — Walk the shot list.** Together, walk through section 5 of the rulebook. For each shot listed, find the actual subject in the venue and stand where the camera would stand. The trainee does not film yet. They identify the shot and the framing.

**0:25-0:35 — What we don't shoot.** Roger walks through section 6 line by line. For each rule, give the real-world example: "the wedding last year where we nearly posted the bride's mother before checking", "the time we caught the till in the back of a pour shot." If you don't have a specific example, John or Roger will supply one. The point is consequence, not theory.

**0:35-0:45 — Q&A and Drive walk-through.** Open the Beercade Australia Shared Drive on the trainee's phone. Show them the `02 Marketing / Content Library` folders. Show them where their footage will go this week.

### Session 1 exit check

Before the trainee leaves, Roger asks three questions verbally. Trainee must answer without notes:

1. Which pillar is a pint pour, and which pillar is a regular sitting at a booth?
2. Name three things from the "do not post" list.
3. Where on Drive does footage from your Tuesday shift go?

If the trainee can't answer all three, run Session 1 again before they start the practical week. Don't paper over it.

## 5. Practical week 1 — Shooting (5 shifts)

**Goal:** The trainee shoots, the trainee does not post.

The trainee's only job this week is to produce raw footage. Roger reviews every piece before any of it touches the public account. Detailed assignments are in `05c_Practical_Exercises.md` — weekly target is 8-10 clips spread across all four pillars, named and filed correctly.

Roger reviews the trainee's footage at the end of each shift, using the review template in `05c_Practical_Exercises.md` section 4. Feedback is written, not just verbal, so the trainee can track what they're being corrected on. The week ends with a 10-minute sit-down where Roger and the trainee look at the week's footage together.

## 6. Session 2 — Craft and posting (45 minutes)

**Goal:** The trainee leaves able to edit, caption, and post under supervision.

**Pre-reading sent the day before:** sections 4, 7, 8, and 9 of `05_Staff_Content_Guidelines.md`.

**Bring:** the trainee's phone, a laptop if they have one, printed copies of `05b_Quick_Reference_Cards.md` cards 4-5.

### Run sheet

**0:00-0:10 — Review the week's best three and worst three clips.** Roger pulls six clips from the trainee's week 1 footage — three that worked, three that didn't. The trainee explains, for each, what was right or wrong. Roger corrects where needed. This is the most useful ten minutes of the program, because the trainee's own footage is the textbook.

**0:10-0:20 — Phone shooting deep dive.** Cover section 4 of the rulebook with the trainee actually holding their phone. Have them frame a pinball machine wrong (too wide, autofocus on the wrong target), then correct it. Then frame a drink with the light behind it, then move and re-frame.

**0:20-0:30 — Captions and voice.** Open the banned words list in section 7.2 of the rulebook. Roger reads out four caption drafts (some real, some made up — use the ones in section 7.3 of the rulebook plus two bad examples). Trainee identifies which break the rules and why. Then trainee writes captions for two of their own week 1 clips, on the spot, while Roger watches.

**0:30-0:40 — The full workflow.** From phone to post. The trainee, using one of their own clips:
1. Names and uploads it to the Raw Footage folder.
2. Edits it (or watches Roger edit it if the trainee doesn't have editing software yet — the venue uses CapCut on shared iPad).
3. Writes the caption.
4. Adds it to the Meta Business Suite scheduled queue.
5. Roger reviews the queued post but does not yet approve it.

**0:40-0:45 — The pre-post checklist.** Trainee recites the five points (pillar, consent, audio, caption rules, tag and location) from memory. If they miss a point, run it again at the end of the next shift until it sticks.

### Session 2 exit check

Roger asks two questions:

1. Walk me through the pre-post checklist without looking at the card.
2. Show me on your phone where you'd find the licensed audio library for a Reel.

Both must be answerable without prompting.

## 7. Practical week 2 — Full workflow (5 shifts)

**Goal:** The trainee shoots, edits, captions, and queues posts. Roger reviews every queued post before it goes live.

Targets and assignments are in `05c_Practical_Exercises.md` section 3. Roger holds the publish button: nothing the trainee queues goes live without Roger clicking through it on the Meta Business Suite review screen.

Stories are the exception — they're live by nature. For week 2, Roger physically watches each Story go up over the trainee's shoulder. From week 3 onwards (after sign-off), Stories can go up without prior approval, but every Story is still subject to the post checklist.

## 8. Competency check (30 minutes)

End of week 2. Format defined in `05d_Competency_Check.md`. Two parts:

1. **Scenario quiz** (20 minutes). Ten written scenarios — the trainee identifies what's wrong with each. Pass mark is 8/10.
2. **Portfolio review** (10 minutes). Roger and trainee look at the two weeks of work together. Roger flags the three posts that best represent each pillar and one piece that should never have been queued.

If the trainee passes, they're signed off to post Stories live without prior approval. Feed and Reels still go through Roger's review queue for another four weeks before unsupervised posting begins.

If the trainee doesn't pass, they re-run whichever week was weakest. No shame in it — the goal is they're producing content that doesn't damage the brand, not that they pass on a deadline.

## 9. Ongoing refresher cadence

Training is not a one-off.

- **Monthly:** Roger reviews the previous month's content with each manager-shift staff member in a 15-minute sit-down. What worked, what didn't. Same template as the week 1 review.
- **Quarterly:** Full rulebook re-read. Anyone who has been posting for a quarter re-reads sections 6 (don't post) and 7 (captions) and verbally confirms with Roger they're still on the rules.
- **Whenever the rulebook changes:** Roger sends the diff in the team chat with a one-line summary. If the change affects what can or can't be posted, it's a full sit-down, not a chat message.

## 10. What to do when a new tool or platform shows up

Instagram changes. TikTok changes. Something new will come along that nobody has trained for. The rule: nobody posts on a new platform from the Beercade account until Roger and John have agreed the platform meets the four pillars and the don't-post rules port over. If a staff member wants to suggest a new platform, they suggest it. They do not post on it.

## 11. Sign-off

When a trainee completes the program, Roger logs the date in `02 Marketing / Content Library / 00_Trained_Staff.md` (single line: name, date passed, sign-off level). Until that line exists, the staff member's name does not go on the Meta Business Suite admin list.

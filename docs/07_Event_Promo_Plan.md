## 1. The pitch in one paragraph

End of financial year is the only night of the corporate calendar where nobody wants to be in a function room. Offices want to blow off steam, ditch the spreadsheet for the night, and be somewhere their CFO will not be tracking the bar bill in real time. Beercade is the perfect venue for that, and offices in Redfern, Surry Hills, and Alexandria do not know we exist yet. EOFY Office Trash Night is two nights of pre-packaged group bookings designed to convert one office into ten reservations across the next six months.

## 2. Why this event, why now

Three reasons.

**It fits the venue without forcing it.** Arcade bars are built for group play. Office teams of 6 to 40 are exactly the size that works with our floor plan, our function form, and the new Bookings calendar. No room rebuild, no menu rebuild, no special staffing.

**EOFY is one of the two biggest function windows of the year** (the other being Christmas, six months out). Booking Beercade for an EOFY team night gets us into the calendar consideration set for the much bigger Christmas booking, where the average spend is 2-3 times higher and the lead time is longer.

**The launch grid covers the cost of acquisition.** We are already producing daily content for the public launch (Day 21, Thu 12 June) and the league (Tue 16 June). Adding EOFY-targeted outreach on top of that costs incremental outbound time, not new ad spend.

## 3. Target customer

**Primary:** office managers, EAs, and "the person who plans the office stuff" in small-to-medium businesses (10-150 staff) located within 2 km of 113 Regent Street, Redfern. The actual decision-maker is rarely the CEO. It is the person whose Outlook calendar is shared with everyone and who has been asked "did we book somewhere for EOFY drinks yet?" four times this week.

**Secondary:** team leads at large companies (CBA, Atlassian, Canva, Westpac, Macquarie) who book independent team events outside the corporate-approved venue list. Often more agile than the central EA function.

**Geography:** Redfern (postcode 2016), Surry Hills (2010), Alexandria (2015), Waterloo (2017), Eveleigh / South Eveleigh, Chippendale (2008). The "walking distance after 6 pm" radius.

**Not the target:** legal firms, accounting partnerships, government departments. They book the same hotel function room every year and our outreach will not move them in one cycle. Save them for Christmas planning starting October.

## 4. The offer — three tiers

Three pre-packaged tiers, priced per head, so the office manager can pick a tier without a back-and-forth on every line item. All tiers include reserved area, machine credits, drinks, and food.

### Tier 1 — Sign-Off (4-8 people, $45 per head)

- 2 hours reserved standing area near the machine wall.
- $20 machine credit per head (loaded onto a group card).
- 2 drink vouchers per head (any tap beer, house wine, house cocktail, or non-alcoholic).
- One large pizza per 4 guests from the venue's regular menu.
- One reserved high-top table for bags and drinks.

### Tier 2 — End of Year (9-20 people, $55 per head)

- 3 hours reserved booth area plus adjacent standing.
- $25 machine credit per head.
- 3 drink vouchers per head.
- Pizza + share boards (proportions calibrated to group size).
- Reserved booth visible from the bar for the duration of the booking.

### Tier 3 — Full Reset (21-40 people, $65 per head)

- 3 hours with the back section of the venue exclusively reserved.
- $30 machine credit per head.
- 4 drink vouchers per head.
- Full share-board food spread (pizzas, fries, sliders, plant-based options).
- Branded "EOFY Trash Night" Polaroid wall (one-off for this event — see section 11).
- Optional add-on: 30 minutes of one-on-one machine coaching from a staff member for the team's chosen "pinball champion of the night." Free with this tier.

### Add-ons (any tier)

- Extra hour of reservation: $200 per booth (booth size depending).
- Custom high-score competition for the team (winner gets a $50 bar tab): $80 set-up fee.
- Trash-the-spreadsheet wall (covered in section 11): free, included as the event hook.

### Overflow

If both nights sell out before Mon 22 June, open a third night on Thu 2 July (post-EOFY) as a "Late Cycle" night. Same packages, same pricing. We do not open Thu 2 July before confirming the first two are tight.

### Hard rules

- No bookings smaller than 4 people. Below 4, it's a normal walk-in.

## 6. Outreach plan

Three channels, sequenced. No paid ads, organic only.

### 6.1 Direct outreach (the main channel)

Roger and John build a list of 80-120 target offices within the geography in section 3. Sources:

- LinkedIn search by location ("People who work at companies near Redfern, Sydney, function: Office Manager / EA / People Ops")
- The "Sydney coworking spaces" directory (Stone & Chalk, Tank Stream Labs, Fishburners, WeWork Pyrmont)
- Local business directories (City of Sydney business listings)
- Foot-traffic survey: Roger walks the surrounding streets one weekday lunchtime, noting offices with visible signage

Two outreach waves.

**Wave 1 — Mon 1 June (3 weeks before Night 1).** Personal LinkedIn message from Roger to the named office manager / EA. No mass email. Hand-typed, two sentences, with the event landing page link. Aim: 40 messages on Day 1, 80 total across the week.

**Wave 2 — Mon 15 June (10 days before Night 1).** Email follow-up to anyone who opened LinkedIn but did not respond. Email goes from `hello@beercade.com.au`. Aim: 60 emails, sent in batches across 3 days.

Conversion target: 15% reply rate on Wave 1, 10% reply-to-book rate on Wave 2. With 120 contacts, that lands roughly 18-20 booked offices for the two nights, averaging 12 heads per booking = 220+ guests across the event. Realistic stretch goal: half that. Realistic floor: 6 bookings, ~70 guests.

## 9. The trash-the-spreadsheet wall (the event hook)

The signature element. Cheap to set up, photographs well, gives the night a name worth printing.

**What it is:** a wall section near the entry covered in butcher's paper. Sharpies in a cup attached by string. Guests are invited on arrival to write one thing they are leaving in this financial year — a project, a client, a quarterly report, a piece of jargon, a colleague's habit. Anonymous. Wall stays up for the duration of the night. At close, Roger lights it on fire in the back alley.

Just kidding. We do not light it on fire. We take a photo of the full wall, post it as a Story the next morning, and recycle it. The "burning" gag stays in the copy but not in the venue.

**Why it works:**

1. It is a participation moment that takes thirty seconds and doesn't slow the night down.
2. It produces good Stories content on its own (close-ups of what people wrote, no faces required).
3. It gives the night a name that is more interesting than "function".
4. The cost is butcher's paper and a $20 pack of Sharpies.

**Operational requirement:** a 2 m x 1.5 m wall section near the entry needs to be cleared of any current signage for the two event nights. Staff briefed not to remove or write on the paper during the night. The wall photographer (whoever is on Pillar D content that night) photographs the wall at three points: 7 pm (mostly empty), 9 pm (filling), 10:30 pm (full).

## 10. Night-of run sheet

Times below are per night. Tier and booking details come from the Bookings calendar synced with the function enquiry form.

### 10.1 Pre-shift (4:30 pm)

- **4:30 pm:** Roger or manager-shift staff arrives. Pulls the booking sheet for the night (printed from the calendar).
- **4:45 pm:** Set up the trash wall — clear the entry wall section, mount butcher's paper, hang the Sharpies cup. Stencil "WHAT WE'RE LEAVING IN FY25/26" at the top in marker. Photograph empty.
- **5:00 pm:** Set up reserved booths with the placeholder cards ("Reserved — [Company]"). Confirm machine credits loaded on group cards (one card per booking, $X total credit pre-loaded).
- **5:15 pm:** Brief any extra staff (if scheduled) on the night's bookings, dietary requirements, and any specific add-ons.
- **5:25 pm:** Last walk-through: lighting, music level, signage, bar stocked, kitchen briefed.

### 10.2 Doors open (5:30 pm)

- **5:30 pm:** Doors open. Walk-in customers seated as normal. Booking arrivals greeted by the door staff member, given group card and drink vouchers per their tier, walked to their reserved booth.
- **5:35 pm:** First booking arrivals start. Door staff hands them a Sharpie and points at the trash wall on the way past.
- **6:00-7:00 pm:** Peak arrival window. Door staff handles the rush; bar runs as normal; kitchen starts food drops at 6:30 pm per pre-orders.
- **7:00 pm:** Photograph the trash wall (early state, partial coverage).

### 10.3 Mid-night (7:00-9:30 pm)

- Bar staff serve drinks against vouchers and group tabs. Drink vouchers are stamped or torn at the point of redemption to prevent double-claims.
- Machine credit cards swiped at the relevant machines. If a group runs out of credit, they can top up at the bar at standard rates.
- Food drops staggered per booking.
- Pillar D content captured per the Staff Content Guidelines — no faces unless verbal yes on the night.
- **9:00 pm:** Photograph the trash wall (filling state).

### 10.4 Wind-down (9:30-11:00 pm)

- Bookings without paid extensions wrap at their reserved end time. Guests can stay as walk-in customers and continue at normal rates.
- **10:30 pm:** Photograph the trash wall (full state).
- **10:45 pm:** Group tabs settled. Any balance over the prepaid deposit charged to the company card on file (signed at booking).
- **11:00 pm:** Normal Thursday/Friday closing pace.

### 10.5 Post-shift

- Trash wall photographed in full (high-res), then removed and stored flat for the next day's Story.
- Group tabs reconciled in the till against the prepaid deposits.
- Bookings calendar updated to "Completed".
- Staff debrief: any spills, no-shows, dietary mishaps, repeat-booker leads.

## 11. Pricing and economics

### 11.1 Per-tier margin (rough)

Per-head price minus inclusions cost, before staff allocation.

| Tier | Price/head | Drink vouchers cost | Food cost | Machine credit cost to venue | Gross margin/head |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sign-Off | $45 | ~$8 (2 vouchers) | ~$5 | ~$8 (some credit unused) | ~$24 |
| End of Year | $55 | ~$12 | ~$8 | ~$10 | ~$25 |
| Full Reset | $65 | ~$16 | ~$10 | ~$12 | ~$27 |

These are estimates. Actual margins depend on the realised tap mix (vouchers used on premium drinks lower the margin) and whether machine credits get fully spent. Conservative estimate per night for a 60-guest mix: $1,500 gross margin before staff allocation and beyond-package walk-up spend.

### 11.2 Beyond-package spend

Historical inner-Sydney venue benchmarks suggest function guests spend an additional $25-50 per head over their package. Conservative estimate for 60 guests x $30 additional = $1,800 in additional bar revenue per night.

### 11.3 Per-night cost outlay

- Trash wall materials: $30 one-off, splits over two nights.
- Pizza and food allocations: covered in the per-head price.
- Bar tab vouchers: covered in the per-head price.
- Extra staff (if any): standard hourly rate, charged to the event if a manager-shift staff is rostered specifically for it.

### 11.4 Cancellation policy

50% deposit non-refundable on confirmation. Balance billed on the night via the group tab. If the booking cancels with more than 14 days' notice, the deposit can be transferred to a future booking within 6 months. Inside 14 days, the deposit is forfeit.

## 12. What can go wrong

### 12.1 Low conversion from outreach

If we hit Wave 1 + Wave 2 and still have only 3 bookings by Mon 15 June, switch the messaging on socials from "EOFY Office Trash Night" to "EOFY Anything Night" — opening the nights to small business owners, freelancers, friend groups, and birthday bookings. The packages and pricing stay the same; only the targeting and copy shift. This is a "rescue mode", not the default.

### 12.2 One booking blocks another

Bookings overlap and one of them runs late, blocking the next group's booth. Mitigation: build a 30-minute buffer between consecutive bookings on the same booth. Confirmed bookings get the booth assignment 72 hours before the night, not on the day.

### 12.3 Drink voucher exploitation

Guests trying to pool vouchers across guests to grab a premium spirit each. Policy: vouchers are per-head, non-transferable, and stamped on use. Bar staff check the stamp before serving. Communicated on the booking confirmation email.

### 12.4 The trash wall gets written on inappropriately

A guest writes something hateful, defamatory, or just stupid on the trash wall. Mitigation: staff sweep the wall every 30 minutes during peak. Marker over anything offensive. The wall is photographed only after the staff sweep, never live.

### 12.5 No-shows

A confirmed booking does not arrive. Deposit covers the venue's exposure. The booth gets opened to walk-in seating at 8 pm if no contact from the booking by then.

### 12.6 Over-booking

We accidentally book more than capacity. Mitigation: capacity is tracked live in the Bookings calendar. The function enquiry form checks against capacity before confirming. Manual bookings (over the phone or in person) must be entered into the calendar by the staff member who took them, immediately.

## 13. Post-event followup

Within 5 working days of each night:

1. **Thank-you email** to each booking contact (`hello@` mailbox, drafted in advance, personalised name/date/tier). One line at the end: "Want to chat about a Christmas booking? Reply and I'll send our spring menu."
2. **Christmas booking soft-open** to anyone who booked EOFY. Christmas spots open to the general public Mon 1 September; EOFY bookers get first access from Mon 18 August.
3. **Post-event review** by Roger and John on Mon 6 July (the Monday after Night 2). One-pager covering attendance, revenue, content, conversion to repeat enquiry, and what to change for the next function-style event (likely Christmas).
